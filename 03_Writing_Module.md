# Writing Module
**Target: Band 7.5–8 | Time: Task 1 = 20 min · Task 2 = 40 min**

---

## How Writing is scored

Every Writing response is marked on **4 criteria**, each worth 25%:

| Criterion | What the examiner asks |
|---|---|
| **Task Achievement** (T2) / **Task Response** (T1) | Did you fully answer what was asked? |
| **Coherence & Cohesion** | Does it flow logically? Are paragraphs clear? |
| **Lexical Resource** | Is the vocabulary varied, precise, and appropriate? |
| **Grammatical Range & Accuracy** | Do you use complex structures correctly? |

**Band 7 descriptors (what you need to achieve):**
- Task Achievement: addresses all parts of the task with a clear position
- Coherence: logically organised, uses cohesive devices flexibly
- Lexical Resource: uses less common vocabulary with some awareness of style
- Grammar: uses a variety of complex structures with frequent error-free sentences

---

## TASK 2 — The Academic Essay

Task 2 is worth **twice as much as Task 1** in your final Writing score. Always prioritise it.

**Word count:** minimum 250 words (aim for 270–300)
**Time:** 40 minutes

---

### The 4 Question Types

| Type | Example prompt | Your job |
|---|---|---|
| **Opinion** | *To what extent do you agree or disagree?* | State and defend your position |
| **Discussion** | *Discuss both views and give your own opinion* | Present both sides + your view |
| **Problem-Solution** | *What are the causes? What solutions can be proposed?* | Identify problems and solutions |
| **Advantages-Disadvantages** | *Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?* | Weigh both sides, give a verdict |

---

### The 4-Paragraph Structure (use this every time)

```
PARAGRAPH 1 — Introduction        (40–60 words)
PARAGRAPH 2 — Body 1              (90–110 words)
PARAGRAPH 3 — Body 2              (90–110 words)
PARAGRAPH 4 — Conclusion          (40–60 words)
```

This structure works for ALL 4 question types. Learn it once, apply it always.

---

### PARAGRAPH 1 — Introduction Formula

**Two sentences only:**
1. Paraphrase the question (do NOT copy it word for word)
2. State your position / thesis clearly

**Template:**
> *[Paraphrase of topic] + [context sentence if needed]. This essay argues that [your position] / While some argue [view A], this essay contends that [your position].*

**Example prompt:**
*"The development of technology has made people's lives more convenient. However, it has also created new problems. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"*

**Band 5 introduction (avoid):**
> *In this essay I will discuss technology. Technology has made life convenient but also created problems. I agree with this statement.*

**Band 7+ introduction:**
> *Rapid advances in technology have undoubtedly transformed daily life, offering greater efficiency and connectivity. However, this essay argues that while the conveniences are significant, the social and psychological challenges technology introduces are equally considerable.*

**Why is it better?**
- Paraphrases the prompt without copying it
- Uses precise vocabulary: *rapid advances, undoubtedly, efficiency, connectivity*
- States a nuanced position in one sentence
- No filler phrases ("In this essay I will discuss...")

---

### PARAGRAPH 2 — Body 1 Formula

**Three-step structure: Claim → Explain → Example**

```
CLAIM:    Topic sentence — your main point for this paragraph
EXPLAIN:  Develop the idea — why / how is this true?
EXAMPLE:  Specific evidence, data, or scenario that proves it
LINK:     (optional) Connect back to the question
```

**Template:**
> *[Claim sentence]. This is because [explanation]. For instance / To illustrate, [specific example]. [Link sentence — optional].*

**Band 5 body paragraph (avoid):**
> *Technology is good for people. It makes life easier. For example, smartphones are very useful. People can use them for many things.*

**Band 7+ body paragraph:**
> *One significant advantage of technological advancement is its capacity to enhance productivity in professional environments. Automation and digital tools allow workers to complete tasks with greater speed and accuracy, reducing the margin for human error. For instance, in the field of electrical engineering, computer-aided design software has dramatically shortened the prototyping cycle, enabling teams to iterate designs in days rather than months. Consequently, technology does not merely add convenience — it fundamentally reshapes the way industries operate.*

**Why is it better?**
- Precise vocabulary: *capacity to enhance, automation, margin for human error, iterate*
- Technical example (relevant to engineering — your field)
- Logical chain: claim → mechanism → evidence → implication
- No vague words like "good", "useful", "many things"

---

### PARAGRAPH 3 — Body 2 Formula

Same structure as Body 1, but arguing the **second point** (or the counterargument if your question requires it).

**For Opinion essays:** Body 2 = your second supporting argument  
**For Discussion essays:** Body 2 = the opposing view, presented fairly  
**For Problem-Solution:** Body 2 = the solution  
**For Advantages-Disadvantages:** Body 2 = the disadvantages

**Transition from Body 1 to Body 2:**
- *Nevertheless, it must be acknowledged that...*
- *On the other hand, critics argue that...*
- *However, a significant drawback of this trend is...*
- *Conversely, there are those who contend that...*

---

### PARAGRAPH 4 — Conclusion Formula

**Two sentences only:**
1. Summarise the argument (do NOT introduce new ideas)
2. Restate your position with slightly different words

**Template:**
> *In conclusion, while [acknowledge the other side briefly], it is clear that [restate your main argument]. [Final thought on implications or recommendation — optional].*

**Band 5 conclusion (avoid):**
> *In conclusion, technology is good and bad. I think it is mostly good. That is my opinion.*

**Band 7+ conclusion:**
> *In conclusion, while technology introduces genuine challenges to social interaction and mental well-being, its contribution to professional efficiency and global connectivity represents a net benefit for modern society. Ultimately, the key lies not in limiting technological progress, but in developing the digital literacy needed to engage with it responsibly.*

After every practice essay, check these before moving on:

**Task Achievement**
- [ ] I addressed ALL parts of the question (not just one aspect)
- [ ] My position is clear from the introduction
- [ ] Each body paragraph has one clear main idea

**Coherence & Cohesion**
- [ ] I used a discourse marker to open each body paragraph
- [ ] Each paragraph has a clear topic sentence
- [ ] My ideas flow logically within each paragraph

**Lexical Resource**
- [ ] I did not repeat the same content word more than twice in the essay

## TASK 1 — Describing Visual Data

**Word count:** minimum 150 words (aim for 170–190)
**Time:** 20 minutes
**Key rule:** You DESCRIBE data. You do NOT explain WHY or give opinions.

---

### The 4 Task 1 Types

| Type | What you do |
|---|---|
| **Line graph** | Describe trends over time |
| **Bar chart** | Compare quantities across categories |
| **Pie chart** | Describe proportions |
| **Table** | Select and compare key data |
| **Process diagram** | Describe stages in a sequence |
| **Map** | Describe changes between two time periods |

---

### The 3-Paragraph Structure

```
PARAGRAPH 1 — Introduction       (1–2 sentences)
PARAGRAPH 2 — Overview           (2–3 sentences) ← MOST IMPORTANT
PARAGRAPH 3+— Key Details        (rest of the words)
```

**The Overview is the most important paragraph in Task 1.**
It states the 2–3 most significant features of the data WITHOUT numbers.
Missing or weak overview = maximum Band 5, regardless of everything else.

---

### PARAGRAPH 1 — Introduction Formula

Paraphrase the task description. Never copy the original wording.

**Original:** *The graph below shows electricity consumption in four countries between 2000 and 2020.*

**Band 7+ introduction:**
> *The line graph illustrates the amount of electricity consumed in four nations over a twenty-year period from 2000 to 2020.*

Changes made: *shows → illustrates / countries → nations / between 2000 and 2020 → over a twenty-year period*

---

### PARAGRAPH 2 — Overview Formula

**Template:**
> *Overall, it is clear that [most striking trend/feature]. While [second notable feature], [contrast or additional observation].*

**Example:**
> *Overall, it is clear that Country A experienced the most dramatic increase in consumption over the period, while Country D remained relatively stable throughout. Notably, all four nations showed higher consumption in 2020 than at the start of the period.*

**Rules for the Overview:**
- No specific numbers or dates
- Covers the WHOLE graph, not one detail
- Uses "Overall" or "In general" to open

---

### Language of Trends (Line Graphs)

**Upward movement:**
- rose / increased / grew / climbed / surged / soared *(strong)* / expanded
- *a rise / an increase / a surge / an upward trend*

**Downward movement:**
- fell / decreased / declined / dropped / plummeted *(dramatic)* / diminished
- *a fall / a decrease / a decline / a drop*

**No change:**
- remained stable / stayed constant / levelled off / plateaued

**Fluctuation:**
- fluctuated / varied / oscillated
- *showed considerable fluctuation*

**Adding degree:**
- dramatically / significantly / sharply / considerably / slightly / marginally / steadily / gradually

**Sentence patterns:**
> *Consumption rose sharply from 200 to 450 units between 2005 and 2010.*
> *There was a gradual decline in output over the following decade.*
> *The figure for Country B fluctuated throughout the period, ending at roughly 300 units.*

---

### Language of Comparison (Bar Charts, Tables, Pie Charts)

**Showing the highest:**
- accounted for the largest share / represented the highest proportion
- *Country A consumed significantly more than the others.*

**Showing the lowest:**
- recorded the smallest figure / had the lowest proportion

**Comparing two items:**
- compared with / in contrast to / whereas / while
- *While Country A showed steady growth, Country C experienced a decline.*
- *Country B consumed twice as much as Country D.*

**Approximating data:**
- approximately / roughly / around / just over / just under / nearly / almost
- *The figure stood at approximately 350 units.*

---

### Process Diagrams — Language of Sequence

> *First, ... / Initially, ...*
> *Then / Subsequently / Following this, ...*
> *Next / At this stage, ...*
> *Finally / Ultimately / In the final stage, ...*
> *The process begins with ... and concludes with ...*

**Key grammar for processes: passive voice**
> *The raw material is heated to 800°C. It is then transferred to the cooling chamber, where it is compressed into sheets.*

---

### Task 1 Self-Assessment Checklist

- [ ] Introduction paraphrases the original — no copied phrases
- [ ] Overview paragraph is present and covers the whole image
- [ ] Overview has NO numbers or specific dates
- [ ] Key data points are selected (not every single number)
- [ ] Numbers are approximated where appropriate
- [ ] No opinion or explanation of WHY (just describe what you see)
- [ ] Trend vocabulary is varied (not "increased" five times)
- [ ] Word count is 170–190

## Practice Essay Prompts — Ordered by Difficulty

### Starter (Week 3–4)
1. *Many people believe that the best way to improve public health is to increase the number of sports facilities. Others believe that this would have little effect and other measures are required. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.*

2. *Some people think that it is better to educate boys and girls in separate schools. Others believe that boys and girls benefit from attending mixed schools. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.*

### Intermediate (Week 5–6)
3. *The increasing use of technology in the workplace has reduced the need for face-to-face interaction between employees. Do the advantages of this development outweigh the disadvantages?*

4. *In many countries, the gap between the rich and the poor is widening. What problems does this cause and what measures can be taken to address the situation?*

### Advanced (Week 7–8)
5. *Some people argue that technological innovation is the most important factor in addressing climate change. To what extent do you agree or disagree?*

6. *As automation and artificial intelligence continue to advance, many traditional jobs will disappear. Some believe this will create new opportunities, while others fear widespread unemployment. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.*

## Sample Band 7.5 Essay — Annotated

**Prompt:** *Some people think that technology has made life more convenient. However, others argue that it has created new problems. To what extent do you agree or disagree?*

---

> **[INTRODUCTION]** Rapid advances in digital technology have fundamentally altered the way people live and work, offering unprecedented levels of convenience. However, this essay argues that while technology delivers genuine benefits, the social and psychological challenges it generates must not be underestimated.

*Note: paraphrases the prompt, states a balanced but clear position, uses precise vocabulary: unprecedented, generates, underestimated*

---

> **[BODY 1 — advantage]** One of the most significant contributions of technology is its capacity to enhance efficiency across virtually every sector of modern life. Automation tools and digital platforms allow individuals to complete complex tasks in a fraction of the time previously required, freeing up hours that can be redirected toward more creative or personal pursuits. In the field of engineering, for example, simulation software now enables designers to test structures under thousands of conditions digitally, eliminating the cost and time of physical prototyping. This acceleration of productivity represents a clear and measurable improvement in quality of life.

*Note: Claim → Explain → Specific example (engineering, your field) → Link. Vocabulary: capacity to enhance, fraction of the time, redirected, acceleration of productivity*

---

> **[BODY 2 — disadvantage]** Nevertheless, the proliferation of digital technology has introduced a range of social and psychological difficulties that deserve serious consideration. Constant connectivity, while convenient, has blurred the boundary between professional and personal time, contributing to rising levels of stress and burnout in many workplaces. Furthermore, the increasing mediation of human interaction through screens has been linked to a decline in deep social bonds and, in some studies, to higher rates of anxiety and loneliness. These are not trivial concerns; they represent structural changes to the way human beings relate to one another.

*Note: Opens with a contrast marker, develops the counterargument with depth. Vocabulary: proliferation, blurred the boundary, burnout, mediation, trivial*

---

> **[CONCLUSION]** In conclusion, while technology has undeniably improved efficiency and opened new possibilities, it has simultaneously created social pressures that affect well-being in ways that were not anticipated. Rather than viewing this as a reason to limit progress, society must invest in the frameworks — educational, legislative, and cultural — needed to harness technology's benefits while mitigating its drawbacks.

*Note: Two sentences, no new ideas, restates position with fresh vocabulary: simultaneously, anticipated, harness, mitigating*
