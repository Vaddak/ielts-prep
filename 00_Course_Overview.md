**Student:** Electronic Engineer, B2 level  
**Start:** May 14, 2026 | **Exam target:** October 2026

---

## Why 7.5–8 is achievable from B2

Your B2 base means you already have the hardest thing: functional English. The gap to 7.5–8 is not "learning English" — it is learning **how IELTS thinks**:

- Expanding academic/technical vocabulary (your main lever)
- Learning the structural formulas each task rewards
- Building stamina and speed under timed conditions
- Eliminating the specific mistake patterns that cost bands

## 20-Week Course Structure

### PHASE 1 — Foundations & Diagnosis (Weeks 1–2)
**Goal:** Understand the exam format deeply. Identify your personal weak points.  
**Daily work:** Study exam mechanics + attempt first diagnostic tasks.

| Week | Focus |
|---|---|
| 1 | IELTS format mastery — rules, timing, scoring, question types |
| 2 | Diagnostic mini-tests (one per section) + gap analysis |

---

### PHASE 2 — Vocabulary Engine (Weeks 3–8, ongoing thread)
**Goal:** Add ~600–800 high-value academic words to your active vocabulary.  
**This runs IN PARALLEL with all other phases** — 10 min/day every day.

| Week | Vocabulary Focus |
|---|---|
| 3–4 | Academic Word List (AWL) — top 200 most frequent words |
| 5–6 | STEM & engineering collocations + paraphrase techniques |
| 7–8 | Discourse markers, hedging language, academic register |
| 9–20 | Ongoing review + new topic-specific vocabulary per week |

---

### PHASE 3 — Section Modules (Weeks 3–14)

#### Module A: Writing (Weeks 3–8) — Highest ROI
Writing is where most B2 candidates leave the most points. Two tasks:

| Week | Writing Focus |
|---|---|
| 3 | Task 2 structure — the 4-paragraph formula |
| 4 | Task 2 arguments — how to develop ideas to Band 7 depth |
| 5 | Task 2 vocabulary — topic-specific word banks (technology, environment, education, work) |
| 6 | Task 1 — describing graphs, charts, maps, diagrams (Academic) |
| 7 | Task 1 — language of trends, comparisons, processes |
| 8 | Writing under time pressure — full timed tasks + self-assessment |

#### Module B: Reading (Weeks 7–11)
| Week | Reading Focus |
|---|---|
| 7 | Question types — matching headings, True/False/NG, sentence completion |
| 8 | Skimming & scanning strategies — where to look, not just what to read |
| 9 | Paraphrase recognition — how IELTS hides the answer in plain sight |
| 10 | Vocabulary-in-context — deducing meaning without knowing the word |
| 11 | Timed full reading practice + common traps |

#### Module C: Listening (Weeks 9–13)
| Week | Listening Focus |
|---|---|
| 9 | Section 1–2 strategies (social/everyday contexts) |
| 10 | Section 3–4 strategies (academic monologue — the hardest) |
| 11 | Spelling, number, and proper noun traps |
| 12 | Predicting answers before the recording plays |
| 13 | Timed full listening practice |

#### Module D: Speaking (Weeks 11–15)
| Week | Speaking Focus |
|---|---|
| 11 | Part 1 — fluency and natural answers (avoid over-rehearsed tone) |
| 12 | Part 2 — the 1-minute monologue structure |
| 13 | Part 3 — abstract discussion, giving opinions with precision |
| 14 | Vocabulary range in speech — synonyms, collocations, avoiding repetition |
| 15 | Full mock Speaking tests |

---

### PHASE 4 — Integration & Exam Simulation (Weeks 15–20)
**Goal:** Build full-exam stamina. Find and close remaining gaps.

| Week | Focus |
|---|---|
| 15 | Full mock exam #1 (timed, all 4 sections) + analysis |
| 16 | Targeted revision of weakest areas from mock #1 |
| 17 | Full mock exam #2 + analysis |
| 18 | Final vocabulary consolidation + Writing revision |
| 19 | Full mock exam #3 — exam conditions |
| 20 | Light review only. Rest. Logistics. Confidence. |

## The 4 Scoring Criteria (Writing & Speaking)

### Writing
| Criterion | What it means for you |
|---|---|
| **Task Achievement** | Did you answer ALL parts of the question? |
| **Coherence & Cohesion** | Does it flow logically? Good paragraph structure? |
| **Lexical Resource** | Varied, precise vocabulary — your growth area |
| **Grammatical Range & Accuracy** | Complex sentences used correctly |

### Speaking
| Criterion | What it means for you |
|---|---|
| **Fluency & Coherence** | Speaks without long pauses, ideas connect |
| **Lexical Resource** | Uses precise words, not just safe/basic ones |
| **Grammatical Range** | Uses complex structures naturally |
| **Pronunciation** | Clear, not necessarily accent-free |

## Key Principle

> The examiner is not testing whether you speak perfect English.  
> They are testing whether you can **perform specific tasks** in English at a high level.  
> Learn the tasks. The score follows.
